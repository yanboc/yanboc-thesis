\chapter{通用任务的特征学习理论}\label{chap-PNAS}

本章将围绕神经网络的特征塌缩 (Neural Collapse, NC) 现象展开, 并探索其背后的特征学习机制. 
特征塌缩由Papyan等\cite{nc}首次发现并总结, 是一种普遍存在于神经网络输出层的几何特征. 
具体而言, Papyan等\cite{nc}研究了分类任务中训练的最终阶段 (Terminal Phase of Training, TPT) , 发现输出层特征在该阶段会坍缩为一个等角紧框架 (Equiangular Tight Frame, ETF) 结构. 


NC 已被证实是跨数据集和网络架构的普遍现象. 
许多先前的研究将这一几何结构特征应用于离分布检测 (10) 、长尾学习 (11) 和对抗训练 (12) 等任务. 
本文重点探讨AT下的神经网络特征. 此处AT指通过特定训练范式增强神经网络对抗鲁棒性的方法 (13) , 具体定义可参阅第1.1节. 已有研究 (12) 发现, 神经网络特征在对抗训练中普遍存在. 然而, 通过AT训练的神经网络与传统训练模型存在显著差异 (14,15) . 由此引出两个核心问题：标准神经网络特征与对抗性神经网络特征的本质区别是什么？这种差异背后的本质特征及其理论依据又是什么？
本文的核心目标在于系统阐释标准神经压缩 (NC) 与对抗神经压缩 (AT) 的差异. 具体而言, 我们通过实证研究揭示了对抗训练 (AT) 对神经压缩几何结构的影响, 并发现了单纯形压缩现象. 研究发现, 在AT的最终阶段, 压缩后的单纯形 ETF 几何尺寸较标准神经压缩显著缩小, 且随着对抗样本扰动半径的增大, 压缩程度呈现递增趋势. 图1直观展示了这一研究发现. 
我们首先通过实证研究证明, 单纯形压缩现象在各类模型和数据集中普遍存在且具有普适性. 在此基础上, 我们从理论层面阐释了自适应训练 (AT) 引发该现象的作用机制. 研究还表明, 随着扰动半径的增大, 压缩程度会显著增强. 特别值得注意的是, 我们的理论预测与第3.3节展示的实验结果高度吻合, 这为我们的实证发现提供了有力佐证. 
本文其余部分结构如下：第1节和第2节分别介绍相关研究及术语. 我们在此呈现实证研究结果.

\section{对抗训练与特征塌缩现象的基础概念}

\subsection{对抗训练}\label{sec-adversarial-training}
我们首先引入对抗样本 (adversarial example) 的概念. 
在图像分类问题中, \textit{对抗样本}$\xa$是一类经过恶意构造的样本, 其在视觉上与干净样本$\x$高度相似, 但会迫使分类器对$\xa$和$\x$做出不同的预测. 
更具体地说, 给定分类器$f$, 对于任意$\x \in \R^d$, 我们称$\xa$为$f$在$\x$处的\textit{$\epsilon$-对抗样本}, 如果$\xa$满足$f(\x) \neq f(\xa)$且$\|\xa - \x\|_p \leq \epsilon$. 
在此定义中, 常数$\epsilon$量化了$\x$与$\xa$之间允许的最大扰动, 以确保两者的视觉相似性, 在文献中通常被称为 \textit{扰动半径}(\textit{perturbation radius}). 
对抗训练 (AT) 最初由Madry等\cite{MMSTV2017towards} 提出, 是一种旨在提升机器学习模型对对抗样本鲁棒性的训练范式. 
AT 的核心思想是利用构造出的对抗样本来训练分类器. 
已有研究\cite{trades}, {wat,mart,fat,eat,robnc} 证明, 经过对抗训练的分类器能够  ``净化''  对抗样本的扰动, 从而比正常训练的分类器具备更强的鲁棒性. 

\subsection{特征塌缩}\label{sec-neural-collapse-setup}

在\S\ref{sec-feature-and-feature-learning} 中, 我们引入了特征提取器的概念：一个神经网络分类器通常由一个特征提取器 (feature extractor) 和一个线性分类器 (linear classifier) 组合而成. 
在本文中, 我们用$\boldsymbol{h}: \R^d \to \R^{\dpr}$来表示特征提取器. 
对于任意输入$\x \in \R^d$, $\h(\x)$在文献中通常被称为最后一层特征 (last layer feature) , 也即\S\ref{sec-feature-and-feature-learning} 中所提到的高级特征. 
这里, $d^{\prime}$表示最后一层特征的维度. 
NC 主要涵盖了以下四个特征：

\begin{definition}[特征塌缩]\label{def-NC}
  给定特征提取器$\boldsymbol{h}: \R^d \to \R^{\dpr}$, 我们称$\h$发生了特征塌缩现象, 如果$\h$满足以下四个性质 (记为\textbf{(NC1)} - \textbf{(NC4)}) ：
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{(NC1)} {\heiti 方差塌缩}：通过计算单个类别$k$中所有特征的平均值, 我们可以得到第$k$类类均值向量 ($k$-th class mean vector) 
    \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-class-mean-vector}
        \boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{k} := \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n\in [N]} \h(\x_{k,n}).
    \end{equation}
    方差塌缩指的是在TPT阶段, 最后一层特征的类内方差趋于$0$, 即：
    \begin{equation}
      \left\|  \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{k} - \h(\x_{k,n}) \right\|_{2}  \to 0 , \quad \forall  k \in [K], n \in [N].
    \end{equation}
    \item \textbf{(NC2)} {\heiti 收敛到广义单纯形等角紧框架}：在TPT阶段, 最后一层特征的类均值向量收敛到定义\ref{def-simplex-ETF}给出的广义单纯形ETF. 为了便于实证验证, Papyan等\cite{nc}进一步引入了以下数值特征来刻画\textbf{(NC2)}：

\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-equiv-converge-to-simplex-ETF}
\begin{aligned}
&\left | \left \| \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{k} - \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{G} \right \|_{2} - \left \| \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{k^{\prime} } - \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{G} \right \|_{2} \right | \to 0, \quad \forall k \neq k^{\prime},\\
&\cos \left ( \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{k}- \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{G}, \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{k^{\prime}} - \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{G}\right )\to-\frac{1}{K-1}  , \quad \forall k\ne k^{\prime},
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
其中 $\boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{G} := \frac{1}{K} \sum_{k=1}^{K}\boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{k} $被成为中心化类均值向量 (centralized class mean vector) . 
公式 \ref{eq-def-equiv-converge-to-simplex-ETF} 表明中心化类均值向量将收敛到一个单纯形ETF. 
\item \textbf{(NC3)} {\heiti 收敛到自对偶性}：在TPT阶段, 类均值向量和线性分类器收敛到彼此的重新缩放版本, 即：
\begin{equation}
  \left\|\frac{\boldsymbol{W}^{\top}}{\|\boldsymbol{W}\|_{F}}-\frac{\dot{\boldsymbol{M}}}{\|\dot{\boldsymbol{M}}\|_{F}}\right\|_{F} \rightarrow 0 ,
\end{equation}
其中 $\dot{\boldsymbol{M}} = \left(\boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{1}-\boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{G}, \boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{2}-\boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{G}, \cdots, \boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{K}-\boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{G} \right) \in \mathbb{R}^{d^{\prime} \times k}$ 被成为中心化类均值矩阵 (centralized class mean matrix) . 

\item \textbf{(NC4)} {\heiti 简化到最近类中心}：在TPT阶段, 分类器收敛到选择与训练类均值最近 (在标准欧氏距离下) 的类别, 即：
\begin{equation}
  \arg \max _{k^{\prime}}\left\langle\boldsymbol{w}_{k^{\prime}}, \boldsymbol{h}\right\rangle+b_{k^{\prime}} \rightarrow \underset{k^{\prime}}{\arg \min }\left\|\boldsymbol{h}-\boldsymbol{\bar{h}}_{k^{\prime}}\right\|_{2}.
\end{equation}
其中 $\tilde{\boldsymbol{\mu}}_{c}=\left(\boldsymbol{\mu}_{c}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_{G}\right) /\left\|\boldsymbol{\mu}_{c}-\boldsymbol{\mu}_{G}\right\|_{2}$ 被成为重新缩放的类均值 (renormalized class mean) . \myqed
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

特征压缩现象刻画了神经网络特征提取器的几何特征. 
许多研究已经证明, 多种不同场景下、不同网络在不同数据集上都观测到了定义\ref{def-NC}中所描述的四个特性, 说明NC是神经网络的TPT阶段会出现的一个普遍现象. 
在这四个特征中, \textbf{(NC2)}是最相关的, 因为它揭示了标准和对抗特征塌缩之间最显著的区别, 即广义单纯形ETF定义的单纯形压缩现象. 
因此, 本文的剩余部分将主要关注\textbf{(NC2)}. 

\begin{definition}[广义单纯形等角紧框架\cite{nc}]
  \label{def-simplex-ETF}
  给定正整数 $d^{\prime} > K$与矩阵
  \begin{equation}
      \W = (\W_1, \W_2, \cdots, \W_K) \in \R^{\dpr \times K}
  \end{equation}
  如果$\W = c_1 \boldsymbol{U} \boldsymbol{M} $成立, 则我们称$\W$为一个广义单纯形等角紧框架. 
  其中
  \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-simplex-ETF-M-in-paper}
      \boldsymbol{M} 
      = \sqrt{\frac{K}{K-1}} 
      \left( \boldsymbol{I}_{K} -\frac{1}{K} \mathbf{1}_{K}  \mathbf{1}_{K}^{\top}  \right) \in \R^{K \times K},
  \end{equation}
  $\boldsymbol{U}\in \mathbb{R} ^{d^{\prime}\times K }$ 是一个半正交矩阵 (partial orthogonal matrix) , 即$\boldsymbol{U^{\top } }\boldsymbol{U} =\boldsymbol{I}$, 且$c_1 \in \R$是一个非零常数. 
  \myqed
  \end{definition}
  值得指出的是, 在NC研究中, 单纯形ETF的定义与数学文献中的定义略有不同 \cite{arXiv:math/0301135,arXiv:1504.00253}. 
  为了消除歧义, 本文中所有关于单纯形ETF的讨论都基于定义\ref{def-simplex-ETF}.

\subsection{单纯形压缩现象}\label{sec-simplex-compression-phenomenon}
本节中, 我们考察对抗训练对最后一层特征表示的影响. 
我们定义类间特征距离的平均值为每个类均值向量与全局均值向量之间的差异, 数学表达为：
\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-P-eps}
\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon} =\frac{1}{K} \sum_{k=1}^{K} \left \|  \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{k} - \boldsymbol{\bar{h} } _{G}  \right \|_{2} .
\end{equation}
我们设置相同的扰动半径$\epsilon$和PGD迭代步数进行实验. 
结果如图\ref{fig:Simplex Collapse}所示 ($L_2$范数的实验结果见附录图S1) . 
我们可以看到, 随着扰动半径的增加, $\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon}$减小. 
这个观察表明单纯形ETF结构的几何尺寸随着扰动半径的增加而减小. 
我们称这种塌缩ETF压缩的现象为单纯形压缩现象. 
单纯形压缩现象的解释是, 对抗训练得到的类均值向量比干净训练得到的类均值向量更相似. 
单纯形压缩现象可以被视为模型鲁棒性的一种特征.

在 AT 中, 超参数的选择对模型的鲁棒性和适应性至关重要. 
具体来说, 选择量化对抗样本和自然样本之间差异的范数是关键. 
$L_\infty$范数关注扰动向量的最大分量, $L_2$范数测量干净样本和对抗样本之间的欧氏距离. 
两者都是常用的范数来刻画对抗扰动. 
此外, 生成对抗样本的迭代过程, 如等式\eqref{eq:advsample}所示, 需要多次迭代才能收敛.

\paragraph{Normalization} 
As the empirically observed geometrical properties of NC require proper rescaling and centralizing, we also adopt the following normalization assumptions:
}
\begin{itemize}
    {\color{red}
    % \item The training data are centralized, i.e., 
    % \begin{equation}
    %     \sum_{k} \x_{k,n} = \mathbf{0}, \ \forall n \in [N].
    % \end{equation}
    \item The training data are symmetric, i.e., we assume
    \begin{equation}
        \left\{
    \begin{aligned}

    \end{aligned}
    \right.
    \end{equation}
    We postpone the definition of $T_0$ to \ref{assumption-tpt-in-papar}.
    \item The similarity within the same class and the dissimilarity between different classes are normalized.

    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $(\x_{k,n_1})^{\top} \x_{k,n_2} \in [1-\rho,1+\rho]$, for all $n_1, n_2 \in [N]$,

    \end{enumerate}

    \item The bias terms in $f$ are fixed to zero, i.e., we can assume that $\bbeta = \0$ after centralization, and $\b = \0$ by choosing a proper translation transformation.
\end{itemize}


\paragraph{TPT}
As mentioned in \ref{sec-related-work}, standard and adversarial NC both occur during TPT.
Here, TPT refers to the training epochs after the training risk has converged to effectively zero.
By definition, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq-exp-loss-function}
    \exp(\ell(f(\x_{k,n}),y_k)) = \frac{\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h(\x_{k,n}) \right)}{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h(\x_{k,n}) \right)}.
\end{equation}
{\color{red}
With \ref{eq-exp-loss-function}, we can characterize TPT as follows.
\begin{assumption}[The terminal phase of training]\label{assumption-tpt-in-papar}
    
    In the remainder of this paper, the \textit{terminal phase of training (TPT)} is characterized as follows.
    For all $k \in [K]$, $l \neq k$, and $n \in [N]$, we denote by $T_0$ the \textit{starting step of TPT} such that
    \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-assumption-TPT-k}
        \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}
        {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)} = 1 - \sigma
    \end{equation}
    and
    \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-assumption-TPT-k1-k2}
        \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_{l} \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}
        {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)} = \frac{\sigma}{K-1}.
    \end{equation}
    In \ref{eq-def-assumption-TPT-k,eq-def-assumption-TPT-k1-k2}, the parameter $\sigma \in (0, 1)$ is used to characterize the precision of training.
    It is worth noting that the training risk turns zero if $\sigma=0$.
    \myqed 
\end{assumption}


The following corollary is immediately after \ref{assumption-tpt-in-papar}.
\begin{corollary}\label{corollary-TPT}
    Let $C_1 > C_2 > 0$ be parameters. 
    If we let $\W_k \h^{\iter{t_i}}(\x_{k,n}) = C_i$ and $\W_{l} \h^{\iter{t_i}}(\x_{k,n}) = -C_{i} (K-1)^{-1}$ for all $k \in [K]$, $l \neq k$, $n \in [N]$ and $i \in \{1, 2\}$, then 
    \begin{equation*}
        \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{t_1\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}
        {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{t_1\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)} 
        > 
        \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{t_2\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}
        {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{t_2\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}.
    \end{equation*}
\end{corollary}

It is worth noting that $\W_k \h(\x_{k,n}) = C$ and $\W_{l} \h(\x_{k,n}) = -C (K-1)^{-1}$ for all $k \in [K]$, $n \in [N]$, and $l \neq k$ implies the convergence of $\h$ to simplex ETF (after normalization).
In this sense, \ref{corollary-TPT} demonstrates the features' better convergence to simplex ETF does not imply a smaller training error.
}

To facilitate a more accurate comparison between standard and adversarial NC, we assume that the iterations for both clean training and AT commence at \( T_0 \), i.e., we require that \( t \geq T_0 \) in \ref{eq-def-GD-AT-in-paper,eq-def-GD-in-paper}.

More specifically, we adopt the following assumption based on \ref{def-simplex-ETF}.
\begin{assumption}[NC3]
We assume that the linear classifier $\W$ has converged to simplex ETF at the beginning of TPT.
Specifically, let $\boldsymbol{M}$ denote the matrix defined in \ref{eq-def-simplex-ETF-M-in-paper}, we assume that
\begin{equation}
    \W = c_1 \boldsymbol{U} \boldsymbol{M} = \sqrt{\frac{K-1}{K}} \left( \boldsymbol{e}_1, \boldsymbol{e}_2, \cdots, \boldsymbol{e}_K  \right) \boldsymbol{M},
\end{equation}
where $\left( \boldsymbol{e}_1, \boldsymbol{e}_2, \cdots, \boldsymbol{e}_K  \right) \in \R^{\dpr \times K}$ is a binary partial orthogonal matrix such that for any $k \in [K]$, only the $k$-th entry of $\boldsymbol{e}_k$ takes $1$ while the other entries take $0$. \myqed
\end{assumption}

Notice that taking $c_1 = \sqrt{\frac{K-1}{K}}$ and $\boldsymbol{U} = \left( \boldsymbol{e}_1, \boldsymbol{e}_2, \cdots, \boldsymbol{e}_K  \right)$ is without loss of generality as the simplex ETFs are invariant under orthogonal transformation.

{\color{red}
\subsection{Main Results}\label{subsec-theoretical-main-results}
For any $k \in [K]$ and $t \geq T_0$, let
\begin{equation}
    \boldsymbol{\bar{h}}^{\iter{t}}_{k} := \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n\in [N]} \h^{\iter{t}}(\x_{k,n})
\end{equation}
be the $k$-th class mean vector at iteration $t$ (cf. \ref{eq-def-class-mean-vector}).
We first validate NC2 under the assumptions in \ref{subsec-assumptions}.

\begin{theorem}[NC2]
Given perturbation radius $\epsilon$, there are constants $\boldsymbol{P}_{0},\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon} \in \R$ such that
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\bar{\h}^{(k)}_k \to \boldsymbol{P}_{0} (K-1){K}^{-1}$ and $\bar{\h}^{(l)}_k \to -\boldsymbol{P}_{0} K^{-1}$ under clean training, and
    \item $\bar{\h}^{(k)}_k \to \boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon} (K-1){K}^{-1}$ and $\bar{\h}^{(l)}_k \to -\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon} K^{-1}$ under AT,
\end{itemize}
for all $k \in [K]$ and $l \neq K$ when $t \to \infty$ and $\rho \to 0$.
\label{theorem-NC2}
\end{theorem}
\ref{theorem-NC2} implies that the class mean vectors converge to simplex ETF in both clean training and AT, validating our empirical results regarding NC2.
The geometric interpretation of $\boldsymbol{P}_{0}$ and $\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon}$ is the simplex norm in \ref{eq-def-P-eps} up to scaling.
Furthermore, we can prove the following result based on \ref{theorem-NC2}.
\begin{theorem}[Simplex compression]
\label{theorem-simplex-compression}
Let $\boldsymbol{P}_{0},\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon} \in \R$ be the constants specified in \ref{theorem-NC2}.
We can prove that
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\boldsymbol{P}_{0} = \boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon}$ when $\epsilon=0$, and
    \item $\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon_1} < \boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon_2}$ for any $\epsilon_1 > \epsilon_2 \geq 0$.
\end{itemize} 
\end{theorem}
\ref{theorem-simplex-compression} demonstrates a negative correlation between the perturbation radius ($\epsilon$) and the simplex norms ($\boldsymbol{P}_{0}, \boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon}$), which further validates the simplex compression phenomenon.
}


\subsection{Intuition and Explanation}\label{subsec-intuition}
The core intuition underlying our analysis is illustrated by 
\ref{fig-intuition}.
Given $k \in [K]$, we prove that $\boldsymbol{h}(\x_{k,n})$ would exhibit faster growth rates along a principal direction decided by $\W_k$ than other directions. 
As the training iterations progress, $\boldsymbol{h}(\x)$ would gradually align more closely with the simplex ETF structure. 
More importantly, we prove that \textbf{the presence of adversarial examples in the training data would weaken the signal from $\x$ and thus reduce the growth rate of $\boldsymbol{h}(\x)$}, thereby inducing the simplex compression phenomenon.

\section{Discussion and Conclusion}\label{sec-conclusion}
Many previous works have leveraged NC to enhance the performance of NNs. 
For instance, \cite{DBLP:conf/cvpr/ZhongCY0QZJ23} proposes a method for semantic segmentation with the constraint of NC2, and \cite{DBLP:conf/iccv/0001SHLW23} explores federated learning inspired by NC. Additionally, NC has been utilized in various tasks across different fields, e.g., out-of-distribution detection \cite{DBLP:conf/icassp/ZhangCJZG24,DBLP:conf/iclr/AmmarBPMF24}, class-imbalanced learning \cite{DBLP:conf/icml/0003HNH24}.
Considering the proven validity of the simplex compression phenomenon through extensive experiments and theoretical analysis, leveraging these insights to enhance the performance of adversarial defenses could further improve the robustness of NNs in the future.

\textcolor{red}{Our experimental results and theoretical analysis exhibit a significant negative correlation between the simplex norm $\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon}$ and the perturbation radius $\epsilon$, which lies at the core of our novelly discovered simplex compression phenomenon.
However, the precise mathematical form of this relationship is beyond the scope of this paper and is open for future studies.}

In conclusion, the present paper focuses on exploring and explaining the difference between standard and adversarial NC.
We observe the simplex compression phenomenon that only occurs under AT.
More specifically, we find that the simplex norm compresses as the perturbation radius grows.
Based on extensive experiments, we validate the universality of our findings across various datasets, models, and AT algorithms. 
We also provide a theoretical explanation for the simplex compression phenomenon.
Under the assumptions in \ref{subsec-assumptions}, we first prove the results regarding NC2 under both clean training and AT by specifying the simplex norms (i.e., $\boldsymbol{P}_{0}$ and $\boldsymbol{P}_{\epsilon}$) under clean training and AT in \ref{theorem-NC2}.
Moreover, we demonstrate a negative correlation between the perturbation radius and the simplex norm in \ref{theorem-simplex-compression}.
Our theoretical results are consistent with the empirical finding, which further validates our novelly discovered simplex compression phenomenon.

\subsection{Proofs for Main Results}\label{subsec-main-proof}

The main goal of our analysis can be summarized as follows.
\paragraph{Main goal} We aim to prove that $\exists C, C^{adv} \in \R$ such that
\begin{itemize}
    \item Under clean training, $\w_k \x_{k,n} \to C (K-1){K}^{-1}$ and $\w_{l} \x_{k,n} \to -C (K)^{-1}$ for all $k \in [K]$ and $l \neq k$ (\ref{lemma-TPT-clean}), and
    \item Under AT, $\w_k \x_{k,n} \to C^{adv} (K-1){K}^{-1}$ and $\w_{l} \x_{k,n} \to -C^{adv} (K)^{-1}$ for all $k \in [K]$ and $l \neq k$ (\ref{lemma-TPT-AT}).
    \item More importantly, we prove that $C > C^{adv}$, which implies the simplex compression phenomenon.
    We also demonstrate a positive correlation between the perturbation radius $\epsilon$ and the compression rate.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Proof Sketch}
\ref{theorem-NC2,theorem-simplex-compression} can be implied by \ref{lemma-TPT-AT,lemma-TPT-clean}. \ref{lemma-principal-entry-of-h} is directly derived from Assumptions \ref{assumption-tpt} and \ref{assumption-NC3}.
The proofs of \ref{lemma-TPT-AT,lemma-TPT-clean} mainly utilized mathematical induction.

As a warm-up, the following corollary can be directly driven from \ref{assumption-tpt}. The proof is thus omitted.
\begin{corollary}
    Let $C_1 > C_2 > 0$ be positive parameters. If  we let $\W_k \h^{\iter{i}}(\x_{k,n}) = C_i$ and $\W_{l} \h^{\iter{i}}(\x_{k,n}) = -C_{i} (K-1)^{-1}$ for all $k \in [K]$, $l \neq k$, and $i \in \{1, 2\}$, then 
    \begin{equation}
        \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{1\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}
        {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{1\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)} 
        > 
        \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{2\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}
        {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{2\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}.
    \end{equation}
\end{corollary}

This corollary demonstrates that the features' better convergence to simplex ETF (after normalization) does not imply a smaller training error.
There is a gap between the minimization of training risk (characterized by $\sigma$) and the formation of simplex ETF (characterized by the entries of $\h$).
The following lemma narrows this gap by providing a quantitative relationship between $\sigma$ and the entries of $\h$.


\begin{lemma}\label{lemma-principal-entry-of-h}
For all $k \in [K]$ and $n \in [N]$, according to \ref{assumption-tpt}, we can obtain that  
\begin{equation}
\h^{(k)}(\x_{k,n}) - \h^{(l)}(\x_{k,n}) =
\relu\left(\left(\w^{\iter{T_0}}_{k}\right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right) - 
\relu\left(\left(\w^{\iter{T_0}}_{l}\right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right)
= \log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1)
\end{equation}
holds for all $l \neq k$ at iteration $T_0$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For all $k \in [K]$ and for any $l \neq k$, \ref{assumption-tpt} implies that
% \begin{equation}
%     \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_{l} \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)} 
%     = \exp \left( 
%         \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) - 
%         \W^{\top}_{l} \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
%         \right) > (K - 1)^2,
% \end{equation}
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_{l} \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)} 
    = \exp \left( 
        \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) - 
        \W^{\top}_{l} \h^{\{T_0\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
        \right) = \frac{(1-\sigma)(K-1)}{\sigma},
\end{equation}
in which
% \begin{equation}
%     \W^{\top}_{k} \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) - \W^{\top}_{l} \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) = - \frac{1}{K} \sum_{k^{\prime} \neq l} \w_{k^{\prime} }^{\top} \x_{k,n} + \frac{K-1}{K} \w_{l}^{\top} \x_{k,n}

\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
    \W^{\top}_{k} \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) - \W^{\top}_{l} \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) =& 
    \frac{K-1}{K} \relu\left(\w_{k}^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right) - \frac{1}{K} \relu\left(\w_{l}^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right) -
    \left(
    \frac{K-1}{K} \relu\left(\w_{l}^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right) - \frac{1}{K} \relu\left(\w_{k}^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right) \right)\\

\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Combine the above two equations, we have
\begin{equation}

\relu\left(\left(\w^{\iter{T_0}}_{l}\right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right)
> \log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1)
\end{equation}
for all $l \neq k$, which completes the proof. 
\end{proof}

The update of $\h$ and $\w$ at $t = T_0$ can be written as
\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-update-h-T0}
    \h^{\iter{t+1},(l)}(\x_{x_{k^{\prime}, n}}) 
    = 
    \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{k^{\prime},n}
    =
    \left(\w^{\iter{t}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{k^{\prime},n} -
    \frac{\eta}{NK} \sum_{n \in [N]} \left(
    \sum_{k \neq l} \left(\frac{\sigma}{K-1} \cdot \left(\x_{k,n}\right)^{\top} \x_{k^{\prime},n} \right) 
    - \sigma \cdot 
    \left(\x_{l,n}\right)^{\top} \x_{k^{\prime},n}
    \right).
\end{equation}
We discuss the relationship between $l$ and $k^{\prime}$ in \ref{eq-def-update-h-T0} as follows.
\begin{itemize}
    \item When $k^{\prime} = l$, we have
    \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k=l-geq}
    \begin{aligned}
    \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n} - \left(\w^{\iter{t}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n}
    &=
    \frac{\eta\sigma}{NK} \x_{l,n}^{\top} \left( \sum_{n^{\prime} \in [N]} \x_{l,n^{\prime}} \right) 
    - 
    \frac{\eta\sigma}{NK} \x_{l,n}^{\top} \left( 
        \sum_{n^{\prime} \in [N]} 
        \sum_{k^{\prime} \neq l}
        \x_{k^{\prime},n^{\prime}} \right)\\
    &\geq
    \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 - \rho)^2 - \frac{\eta\sigma(K-1)}{K}\rho(1+\rho)\\
    &=
    \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 - \rho)^2 + O(\rho).
    \end{aligned}
    \end{equation}
    Similarly, we can obtain
    \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k=l-leq}
    \begin{aligned}
    \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n} 
    -
    \left(\w^{\iter{t}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n}
    &\leq  
    \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 + \rho)^2 + \frac{\eta\sigma(K-1)}{K}\rho(1+\rho)\\
    &=
    \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 + \rho)^2 + O(\rho).
    \end{aligned}
    \end{equation}
    \item Otherwise, when $k^{\prime} \neq l$, we have
    \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k-neq-l-leq}
    \begin{aligned}
        \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n} - \left(\w^{\iter{t}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n}
        &=
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{NK(K-1)} \x_{l,n}^{\top} \left( \sum_{n^{\prime} \in [N]} \x_{l,n^{\prime}} \right) 
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{NK(K-1)} \x_{l,n}^{\top} \left( 
            \sum_{n^{\prime} \in [N]}
            \sum_{k \neq l,k^{\prime}}
            \x_{k,n^{\prime}} \right)
        +
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{NK} \x_{l,n}^{\top} \left( \sum_{n^{\prime} \in [N]} \x_{k^{\prime},n^{\prime}} \right)
        \\
        &\leq
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1-\rho)^2
        +
        \frac{\eta\sigma(K-2)}{K(K-1)} \rho(1+\rho)
        +
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} \rho (1+\rho)\\
        &= 
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1-\rho)^2
        + O(\rho).
    \end{aligned}
    \end{equation}
    and
    \begin{equation}\label{eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k-neq-l-geq}
    \begin{aligned}
        \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n}
        -
        \left(\w^{\iter{t}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n}
        &\geq
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1+\rho)^2
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma(K-2)}{K(K-1)} \rho(1+\rho)
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} \rho (1+\rho)\\
        &=
        -
        \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1+\rho)^2 + O(\rho).
    \end{aligned}
    \end{equation}
\end{itemize}
Now, consider the output of the linear layer at iteration $t+1$.
For any $k, l \in [K]$, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq-output-T+1}
\begin{aligned}
    \W^{\top}_l \h^{\{t + 1\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
    &= 
    \frac{K-1}{K} \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{k,n} 
    - 
    \frac{1}{K} \sum_{k^{\prime} \neq l} \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_{k^{\prime}} \right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\\
    &= \W^{\top}_l \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
    +
    \frac{K-1}{K} \left(
    \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n} - \left(\w^{\iter{t}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n} 
    \right)
    -
    \frac{1}{K} \sum_{k^{\prime} \neq l}
    \left(
    \left(\w^{\iter{t+1}}_{k^{\prime}} \right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}
    -
    \left(\w^{\iter{t}}_{k^{\prime}} \right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}
    \right).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
The following bounds are derived from \ref{eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k=l-geq,eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k=l-leq,eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k-neq-l-geq,eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k-neq-l-leq}.
When $k=l$, the growth of \ref{eq-output-T+1} can be bounded by
\begin{equation}
\left\{
\begin{aligned}
\W^{\top}_k \h^{\{t + 1\}}(\x_{k,n}) - \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
&\leq
\frac{K-1}{K} \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 + \rho)^2 + O(\rho) \right) 
+ 
\frac{1}{K} \cdot (K-1) \cdot \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1+\rho)^2
+ O(\rho) \right) \\
\W^{\top}_k \h^{\{t + 1\}}(\x_{k,n}) - \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
&\geq
\frac{K-1}{K} \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 - \rho)^2 + O(\rho) \right) 
+ 
\frac{1}{K} \cdot (K-1) \cdot \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1 - \rho)^2
+ O(\rho) \right),
\end{aligned}
\right.
\end{equation}
and for $l \neq k$, the similar bounds are as follows.
\begin{equation}
\left\{
\begin{aligned}
\W^{\top}_l \h^{\{t + 1\}}(\x_{k,n}) - \W^{\top}_l \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
&\leq
- \frac{K-1}{K}  \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1-\rho)^2
+ O(\rho) \right) 
+ 
\frac{K-2}{K} \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1+\rho)^2 + O(\rho) \right) 
-
\frac{1}{K} \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 - \rho)^2 + O(\rho) \right)
\\
\W^{\top}_l \h^{\{t + 1\}}(\x_{k,n}) - \W^{\top}_l \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) 
&\geq
- \frac{K-1}{K}  \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1+\rho)^2
+ O(\rho) \right) 
+ 
\frac{K-2}{K} \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K(K-1)} (1-\rho)^2 + O(\rho) \right) 
-
\frac{1}{K} \left( \frac{\eta\sigma}{K} (1 + \rho)^2 + O(\rho) \right)
\end{aligned}
\right.
\end{equation}
We check the principal entry of $\h$ and establish bounds in a similar style to \ref{lemma-principal-entry-of-h}.
For any $k \in [K]$ and $l\neq k$, we have
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
&\relu\left(\left(\w^{\iter{T_0+1}}_{k}\right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right) - 
\relu\left(\left(\w^{\iter{T_0+1}}_{l}\right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right)
\\
\geq& \log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1) + 
\frac{\eta\sigma(K+2)}{K^2} (1 - \rho)^2
- 
\frac{\eta\sigma(K-2)}{K^2(K-1)} (1+\rho)^2 
+ O(\rho) \\
=& \log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1) + \frac{\eta \sigma(K^2-2)}{K^2(K-1)}(1 - \rho)^2 + O(\rho).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
\relu\left(\left(\w^{\iter{T_0+1}}_{k}\right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right) - 
\relu\left(\left(\w^{\iter{T_0+1}}_{l}\right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}\right)
\leq \log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1) + \frac{\eta \sigma(K^2-2)}{K^2(K-1)}(1 + \rho)^2 + O(\rho).
\end{equation}
Denote
\begin{equation}
    C_1 = \frac{\eta (K^2-2)}{K^2(K-1)}.
\end{equation}
The updated training risk can be characterized by $\sigma^{\iter{T_0+1}}$ that satisfies
\begin{equation}
    \log\left(\left(\sigma^{\iter{T_0+1}}\right)^{-1} - 1\right) = \log\left(\sigma^{-1} - 1\right) + C_1 \sigma (1 \pm \rho)^2 + O(\rho), 
\end{equation}
which can be simplified to
\begin{equation}
   \sigma^{\iter{T_0+1}} = \left(\left(\sigma^{-1} - 1\right) \exp\left(C_1 \sigma (1 + \tilde{\rho})^2 + O(\rho)\right) + 1\right)^{-1}.
\end{equation}
Back to the main goal of this lemma. 
We have proved \ref{eq-def-assumption-TPT-k-general-t-in-lemma,eq-def-assumption-TPT-k1-k2-general-t-in-lemma} when $t=T_0$.
Now, for any $t > T_0$, observe that 
\ref{eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k=l-geq,eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k=l-leq,eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k-neq-l-geq,eq-def-update-h-T0-bound-k-neq-l-leq} still hold for $\sigma^{\iter{t}}$ defined in \ref{eq-def-sigma-t-clean}.
We immediately obtain \ref{eq-def-assumption-TPT-k-general-t-in-lemma,eq-def-assumption-TPT-k1-k2-general-t-in-lemma} for all $t \geq T_0$ by induction.
The feature at iteration $T$ can be calculated by taking the following sum
\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-update-h-k=l}
\begin{aligned}
\left(\w^{\iter{T}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n} 
=
\left(\w^{\iter{T_0}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{l,n} 
+
\sum_{T_0 \leq t \leq T} \frac{\eta\sigma^{\iter{t}}}{K} (1 + \tilde{\rho})^2 + O(\rho)
\to \sum_{T_0 \leq t \leq T} \frac{\eta\sigma^{\iter{t}}}{K} (1 + \tilde{\rho})^2.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
for $l=k$, and 
\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-update-h-k-neq-l}
    \left(\w^{\iter{T}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}
    =
    \left(\w^{\iter{T_0}}_l \right)^{\top} \x_{k,n}
    -
    \sum_{T_0 \leq t \leq T} \frac{\eta\sigma^{\iter{t}}}{K(K-1)} (1+\tilde{\rho})^2 + O(\rho) 
    \to -\frac{1}{K-1} \sum_{T_0 \leq t \leq T} \frac{\eta\sigma^{\iter{t}}}{K} (1 + \tilde{\rho})^2.
\end{equation}
for $k \neq l$ as $T \to \infty$ and $\rho \to 0$.
We complete the proof by combining \ref{eq-def-update-h-k=l,eq-def-update-h-k-neq-l}.
\end{proof}



\begin{lemma}[The terminal phase of AT]\label{lemma-TPT-AT}
For all $k \in [K]$, $n \in [N]$, and $t \geq T_0$, we have
\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-assumption-TPT-k-general-t-AT-in-lemma}
    \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_k \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k,n}) \right)}
    {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k_2,n}) \right)} = 1 - \sigma^{\iter{t}}_{adv}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-assumption-TPT-k1-k2-general-t-AT-in-lemma}
    \frac{\exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k_1} \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k_2,n}) \right)}
    {\sum_{k^{\prime}} \exp \left( \W^{\top}_{k^{\prime}} \h^{\{t\}}(\x_{k_2,n}) \right)} 
    = \frac{\sigma^{\iter{t}}_{adv}}{K-1},
\end{equation}
in which $\sigma^{\iter{T_0}}_{adv}$ can be obtained by iterating with $\sigma^{0} = \sigma$ and
\begin{equation}\label{eq-def-sigma-adv-at-T0}
    \sigma^{\tau+1} = \left(\left(\left(\sigma^{\tau}\right)^{-1} - 1\right) 
    \exp\left(
        -\frac{\epsilon \left(\sigma^{\tau}\right) K}{K-1} \left(\|\w_{k}\|^2 - \w_k^{\top} \w_l
        \right) 
    + O(\rho)\right) + 1\right)^{-1} > \sigma^{\tau}.
\end{equation}
to the maximum PGD step $\tau=T_a$, and
\begin{equation}
   \sigma^{\iter{t+1}}_{adv} = \left(\left(\left(\sigma^{t}\right)^{-1}_{adv} - 1\right) \exp\left(\frac{\eta (K^2-2)}{K^2(K-1)} \cdot \left(\sigma^{t}\right)_{adv} (1 + \tilde{\rho})^2 + O(\rho)\right) + 1\right)^{-1}.
\end{equation}
More importantly, the feature $\h$ at iteration $T$ satisfy
\begin{equation}
    \frac{\h^{(k),\iter{T}} (\x^{adv}_{k,n}) }{\h^{(l),\iter{T}} (\x^{adv}_{k,n})} \to -(K-1)
\end{equation}
for any $k \in [K]$ and $l \neq k$ as $T \to \infty$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Proof of \ref{lemma-TPT-AT}]
The main difference between \ref{lemma-TPT-clean,lemma-TPT-AT} lies in the characteristic of adversarial examples.
It suffices to prove \ref{eq-def-sigma-adv-at-T0} at iteration $t = T_0$.
The remainder of the proof is similar to that of \ref{lemma-TPT-clean}.
Combining \ref{assumption-tpt} and \ref{eq-def-gradient-based-attack-itertion,eq-nabla-x-l}, we can obtain that
\begin{equation}\label{eq-PGD-attack-iteration-1}
\begin{aligned}
    \x_{k,n}^{1} &= \x_{k,n} + \left(
    \sum_{l \in [K]} \epsilon \left(
    \frac{\exp\left( \o^{(l)} \left( \x_{k,n} \right) \right) }{\sum_{j \in [K]} \exp \left( \o^{(j)} \left( \x_{k,n} \right) \right) } - \delta_{lk}\right) 
    \left(\nabla_{\x} \h(\x_{k,n})\right)^{\top} \W_l \right) \\
    &= \x_{k,n} + \left(
    \sum_{l \in [K]} \epsilon \left(
    \frac{\exp\left( \o^{(l)} \left( \x_{k,n} \right) \right) }{\sum_{j \in [K]} \exp \left( \o^{(j)} \left( \x_{k,n} \right) \right) } - \delta_{lk}\right) 
    \left(
        \frac{K-1}{K} \cdot \1\left((\w_l)^{\top}\x_{k,n}\right) \w_l - \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k^{\prime} \neq l} \1\left((\w_{k^{\prime}})^{\top}\x_{k,n}\right) \w_{k^{\prime}}
    \right)
    \right)
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
By discussing the second term in \ref{eq-PGD-attack-iteration-1}, we have

\begin{equation}
\x_{k,n}^{1} = \x_{k,n} -
\epsilon\sigma \left( 
    \left(
        \frac{K-1}{K} \cdot \w_k - \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k^{\prime} \neq k} \w_{k^{\prime}}
    \right)
-
\sum_{l \neq k} \frac{1}{K-1} \cdot 
    \left(
        \frac{K-1}{K} \cdot \w_l - \frac{1}{K}\sum_{k^{\prime} \neq l} \w_{k^{\prime}}
    \right)
\right).
\end{equation}
By arranging the terms by $\w_l$ (and utilizing the symmetry of $l \neq k$), we have
\begin{equation}
\x_{k,n}^{1} = \x_{k,n} -
\epsilon\sigma 
\left( 
    \w_k - \frac{1}{K-1}\sum_{l \neq k}\w_l
\right).
\end{equation}
Again, we check the principal entry of $\h$ after a one-step adversarial attack on $\x$.
For any $k \in [K]$ and $l\neq k$, we have
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
&\relu\left(\left(\w_{k}\right)^{\top} \x^{1}_{k,n}\right) - 
\relu\left(\left(\w_{l}\right)^{\top} \x^{1}_{k,n}\right)
\\
\geq& \log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1) - \epsilon \sigma (\w_k - \w_l)^{\top} \left( 
    \w_k - \frac{1}{K-1}\sum_{l \neq k}\w_l
\right)
\\
=&\log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1) - \epsilon \sigma (\w_k - \w_l)^{\top} \left( 
    \frac{K}{K-1} \w_k + O(\rho)
\right) \\
=& \log(K-1) + \log(\sigma^{-1} - 1) - 
   \frac{\epsilon \sigma K}{K-1} \left(\|\w_{k}\|^2 - \w_k^{\top} \w_l\right) +O(\rho).
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
It can be observed that the training risk increases after the gradient step, which is due to the nature of the adversarial example.
The updated training risk can be characterized by $\sigma^{\iter{T_0+1}}$ that satisfies
\begin{equation}
\log\left(\left(\tilde{\sigma}^{1}\right)^{-1} - 1\right) = \log\left(\sigma^{-1} - 1\right) - 
   \frac{\epsilon \sigma K}{K-1} \left(\|\w_{k}\|^2 - \w_k^{\top} \w_l\right) +O(\rho), 
\end{equation}
which can be simplified to
\begin{equation}
    \sigma^{1} = \left(\left(\sigma^{-1} - 1\right) 
    \exp\left(
        -\frac{\epsilon \sigma K}{K-1} \left(\|\w_{k}\|^2 - \w_k^{\top} \w_l
        \right) 
    + O(\rho)\right) + 1\right)^{-1}.
\end{equation}
We prove the lemma by induction.
\end{proof}
